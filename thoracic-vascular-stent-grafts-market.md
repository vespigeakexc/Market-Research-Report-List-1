Plastic thoracic vascular stent grafts are often made from biocompatible materials, offering flexibility, reduced risk of corrosion, and ease of implantation. In contrast, metal thoracic vascular stent grafts are typically constructed from durable materials like stainless steel or cobalt-chromium, providing superior strength and structural support. Both types aim to treat vascular conditions by reinforcing blood vessels and improving blood flow in the thoracic region.</p></p>

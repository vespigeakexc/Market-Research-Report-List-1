<p><p>Thermoelectric coolers (TECs) are solid-state devices that utilize the Peltier effect to transfer heat, making them popular for temperature control applications. The market is categorized into single-stage and multistage modules. Single-stage modules consist of one thermoelectric junction, suitable for moderate cooling needs. In contrast, multistage modules feature multiple junctions stacked together, allowing for more significant temperature differentials, making them ideal for applications requiring deeper cooling, such as in laser and sensor systems, where precise thermal management is critical.</p></p>

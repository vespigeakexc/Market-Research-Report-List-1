<p><p>The Severe Breathing Care Ventilator market is categorized into two main types: invasive and non-invasive ventilators. Invasive ventilators require intubation, delivering airflow through a tube inserted into the trachea, often used in critical care settings for patients with severe respiratory failure. Non-invasive ventilators, on the other hand, provide respiratory support through face masks or nasal interfaces, suitable for conditions like COPD or sleep apnea, offering benefits such as reduced risk of infection and increased patient comfort.</p></p>
